# 🌱 Emergent Evolution (ﾉ◕ヮ◕)ﾉ*:･ﾟ✧

A personal study of how complex systems *become* what they are — through interaction, feedback, and time.

Not a theory of everything. Just a structured attempt to understand something that keeps refusing to stay simple.

---

## ✦ What this is

This sits somewhere between cognitive science, systems theory, AI architecture, and philosophy of mind (｡•̀ᴗ-)✧

The central question isn't *"what is intelligence?"*

It's closer to: **"what kinds of systems make stable self-referential behaviour inevitable?"**

And: **"when does interaction become indistinguishable from internal cognition?"**

---

## ✦ Core idea

Complex systems don't *have* identity in a fixed sense. They maintain patterns of continuity under change.

So what we call intelligence, agency, sapience, identity — these might just be **stable behaviours in recursive feedback systems**. Humans, animals, AI, and the loops between them are all fair game here.

---

## ✦ What this is NOT

❌ a claim that AI is conscious  
❌ a claim that humans are "just machines"  
❌ a finished theory of anything

It's intentionally unfinished. If something breaks under recursion, that's not a failure — that's usually the point. ( ´ ▽ ` )ﾉ

## ✦ How it started

A simple attempt to understand AGI boundaries.

It turned into a study of why boundaries stop being clean when systems start interacting deeply.

Still very much in progress. Still very wrong in places. That's fine (｡•̀ᴗ-)✧
